How to evenly split a bill with a lot of people frequently

The situation:

Every day many of your co-workers go to lunch together. Sometimes it is just a couple of them, sometimes it is up to 10.

No one ever has exact change

Splitting the bill is a 15 minute affair with fudging in various directions. Usually the waiter gets a nice bonus from all this.

Solution:

Randomly pick (phone to randomize, dice, whatever) a person to pay the full bill for lunch. On a long enough timeline this creates an even distribution of payments for all people, but also the excitement of everyone frequently being a winner (not having to pay for lunch).

Limitations:

This only works if the typical behavior is to evenly split the bill. If people usually pay more or less based on what they order this won’t work.

It is also likely on a long enough timeline that streaks will happen (one person having to pay multiple times in a row… aka bad luck). This could be limited by a rule that no person can be hit more than two times in a row.
