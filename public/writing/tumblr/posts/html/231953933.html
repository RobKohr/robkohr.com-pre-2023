US - The Saudi Arabia of Coal

These are some random facts from an FT article I read today.

50% of electricity is produced from coal

Workers at the largest coal mine in the world (North Antelope Rochelle) work 12 hours shifts with microwaves, refridgerators, etc in the trucks to keep the operation going 24 hours a day without interruption. Every day 15,500 tones are loaded up on trains from that mine, and it supplies 40% of the US’s coal needs.

This mine has roughly 100-150 years worth of production based on current output. The US is estimated to have several hundred years’ worth of coal to be recovered.

It is estimated that the US will emit 7,550m tons of CO2 a year by 2030, which is 14% more than 2005 levels.

“Barack Obama, president, from a coal-mining state of Illinois, backs continued coal use. He has said if the US can put a man on the moon, it can find a way to capture and store carbon.”

I have my doubts that there is enough push to really make that a reality.

To put carbon capture on existing coal plants woudl push operating costs up by 80%. And as Paul Forester states “If you push 20 years’ worth of CO2 in the ground and it leaks out, your back 30 years. We need it to stay there forever.”

I could imagine us going full on clean-carbon energy producing, just to have the carbon leach out over the next hundred years, and be worse off then we are.

Source: Financial Times November, 3 2009 page 5
