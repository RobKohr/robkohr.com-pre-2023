The Problem

I use an iPhone as really an iTouch (I dislike ATT) that is used mostly to listen to podcasts in the car. One problem I have with the way podcasts are handled in the iphone is that they are all in what amounts to separate playlists, so you need to do a lot of navigation to jump to each of your newest podcasts. Not a good thing when you are driving on the highway.

The Goal

Ideally, I just want to press play, and start driving, and have the ipod play the newest unplayed podcasts.

The trick, set up an automated playlist in itunes, and have the ipod synch to that. When I am on the road, I go to that playlist, hit play, and I am done.

Steps to set up

  1. Open itunes
  2. File->new smart playlist
  3. Call it something like “New Podcasts”
  4. Use the following settings:
  5. Save
  6. click on your ipod/iphone
  7. Click on the “music” tab (not the podcasts one)
  8. Add the smart playlist you created
  9. sync… and you are done

In the ipod itself, you just go to the playlist, and it will have the newest stuff in it.

I set my time limit on the smart playlist to 7 hours, as this is how much I expect to listen to my ipod between syncs (7 days of 1 hour commutes).
