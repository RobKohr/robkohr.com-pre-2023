Greed is not just a human instinct, but a nearly universal biological instinct. It is also a major driver of the economy. Fighting against greed is not just futile, but counter-productive.

The key is that greed needs to be directed at positive results that do not impinge upon other's rights to life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.

I feel that contributing money to a political campaign by any entity (individual or corporate) almost the same as bribery. As you say, certain folks (AKA the Supreme Court) have a different viewpoint.

Capitalism is fantastic, but as you said unchecked capitalism is not. For example, cutting down all of the forest lands to make a buck would be a bad thing. Also, setting up a system where incredibly risky loans are backed by government funds is also really bad (and comes about from a government beholden to corporate interests).

Fortunately broadcast television is a dying medium, and with it the major way that political campaigns have spent their money. Finding unpaid-for information about candidates is becoming more common through the internet, which hopefully will make fundraising (aka sanctioned bribery) less relevant.

In the old days people found out a lot about candidates through their social networks (real ones). Today it is becoming more about virtual ones.
